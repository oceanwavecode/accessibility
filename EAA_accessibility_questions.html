EAA European Accessability Act

The European Accessibility Act (EAA) will be implemented June 28, 2025. Will your organisation's digital products and services meet the requirements in time?

You may find the following user interface and functionality design questions useful (questions refer to Section I of Annex I):

  • Do your products' user interfaces support multiple sensory channels? Vision, Auditory, Speech, Touch.
  • Do the user interfaces of your products provide an alternative to speech output/vocal input for communication, operation control, and orientation?
  • Do visual elements of your products support flexible magnification, brightness, and contrast?
  • Can assistive devices be used to navigate the visual elements of your products?
  • Do your products provide an alternative to colour/audio when colour/audio is used to indicate an action, require a response, or identify elements?
  • Do your products provide volume, speed, and audio clarity options (e.g. reduce audio of other applications)?
  • Do your products ensure there are no features that require simultaneous controls?
  • Does your product include content that could trigger photosensitive seizures?
  • Does the use of accessibility features ensure the user's privacy?
  • Does your product solely rely on biometric identification? If so, you will need to provide an alternative.
  • Are there features within your products that move too fast for some users to interact with?
  • Do your products provide software and hardware for interfacing with assistive technologies?
  • Is your support team able to provide information about the accessible capability of your products and compatibility with assistive technologies?
  • Do you engage with users who rely on accessible product features to analyse quantitative and qualitative data to improve product accessibility?
